Sudha Murthy

Author • Philanthropist • Teacher • Inspiration

Sudha Murthy stands as one of India's most distinguished luminaries, weaving together the threads of literature, education, and social transformation. As the chairperson of the Infosys Foundation and a celebrated author, she has touched countless lives, embodying the rare harmony of extraordinary success and profound compassion.

Literary Legacy

Through her collection of over 24 books across multiple languages, Sudha Murthy has masterfully captured the essence of human experience. Her works, including the beloved "How I Taught My Grandmother to Read" and "Wise and Otherwise," continue to inspire readers worldwide.

Humanitarian Vision

Her philanthropic journey through the Infosys Foundation has transformed countless lives, pioneering initiatives in healthcare, education, and rural development across India, embodying her belief in compassionate action.

Educational Pioneer

Breaking barriers as TELCO's first female engineer and later as a distinguished computer science professor, she has inspired generations of women to pursue their dreams in technology and engineering.

Visionary Leadership

At the helm of the Infosys Foundation, she has orchestrated transformative initiatives in public healthcare, art preservation, and rural development, leaving an indelible mark on India's social landscape.

Her Enduring Legacy

Sudha Murthy's remarkable journey exemplifies the perfect synthesis of achievement and empathy. From breaking gender barriers in engineering to becoming one of India's most beloved authors and philanthropists, she has shown that success and compassion can flourish together. Her legacy continues to inspire generations, proving that with unwavering determination and a generous heart, one individual can illuminate countless lives.
